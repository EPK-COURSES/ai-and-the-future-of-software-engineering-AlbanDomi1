# Personal Reflection

Before researching this topic, I believed that AI would mainly affect software engineering by helping developers write code faster. However, after reading articles, surveys, and industry reports, I realized that the impact of AI is much broader. AI is changing how developers learn, solve problems, test software, create documentation, and collaborate with others.

What surprised me most was that many professional developers still do not fully trust AI-generated code. Before doing this research, I assumed that AI tools were becoming accurate enough to solve most programming problems automatically. However, I learned that AI can still generate incorrect code, security vulnerabilities, and solutions that do not fully match project requirements. This showed me that human verification remains extremely important.

One concern I have is that some students and junior developers may become too dependent on AI tools. If developers rely on AI without understanding programming fundamentals, they may struggle when something goes wrong. AI can provide answers quickly, but it cannot replace knowledge of algorithms, databases, networking, software architecture, and debugging. I believe this is especially important for students who are still building their technical foundations.

My perspective is also influenced by my own experience. As a Software Engineering student, I have completed internships and have been involved in developing real software projects outside of university. Through these experiences, I have noticed that writing code is only one part of software development. A large amount of time is spent understanding requirements, planning features, debugging problems, communicating with others, and making design decisions. These are areas where AI can assist, but where human judgment is still essential.

At the same time, I see many opportunities. AI can significantly increase productivity and help developers learn new concepts faster. During my studies, internships, and personal development projects, I have already used AI tools to understand unfamiliar topics, troubleshoot errors, and improve my workflow. These tools can save time and allow developers to focus on more important tasks rather than repetitive coding work.

After completing this research, I believe that the most valuable software engineers will not necessarily be the programmers who write code the fastest. Instead, they will be the engineers who understand systems, communicate effectively, think critically, and know how to use AI responsibly. Skills such as problem solving, system design, testing, and teamwork will continue to be essential because they require human judgment, experience, and an understanding of real-world situations.

Personally, I plan to adapt by continuing to strengthen my software engineering fundamentals while learning how to use AI effectively. I want to continue gaining practical experience through internships and software development projects while improving my knowledge of software architecture, databases, APIs, testing, and deployment. I believe that combining strong technical skills with AI-assisted development will make me a more effective software engineer in the future.

Overall, this research changed my perspective on AI. I no longer see it as a tool that might replace software engineers. Instead, I see it as a powerful assistant that can improve productivity when used correctly. The engineers who will remain valuable in the AI era are those who continue learning, understand technology deeply, and use AI as a tool rather than a replacement for their own knowledge and judgment.
